# Journal Target Decision

## Selected Journal

- `Malaria Journal`
- Article type: `Research`

## Why This Is The Best First Submission

- The official scope explicitly includes malaria `operational research`, `health system, policy and planning issues`, and `intervention studies`.
- The current article is malaria-specific and focused on how malaria RCTs in Africa can inform leaner future trial delivery, so the disease-specific framing is an asset rather than a distraction.
- `Trials` may be slightly cheaper on some current pricing pages, but the official editorial timing shown for `Malaria Journal` is materially faster.
- Initial submissions are flexible in format, which reduces packaging friction for a data-and-artifact-heavy first submission.

## Current Official Signals

- `Malaria Journal` APC: `£2190 / $2890 / €2490`.
- `Malaria Journal` speed: median `8` days to first editorial decision and `125` days to acceptance.
- `Trials` official pages currently show slower editorial timing than `Malaria Journal`, with first-decision medians displayed in the `41` to `67` day range depending on page.

## Backup Journal

- `Trials`
- Use if `Malaria Journal` considers the paper too trial-methods focused and not sufficiently disease-facing.
- `Trials` remains a strong backup because its scope explicitly includes `research into trial processes` and lessons for future trial design and reporting.

## Positioning Note

This package is not a conventional intervention-effect meta-analysis. It is best positioned as malaria-focused operational research using public registry data to identify transferable RCT design templates for African settings.
