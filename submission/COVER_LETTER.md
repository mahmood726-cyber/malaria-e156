# Cover Letter Draft For Malaria Journal

Dear Editors,

Please consider our Research article, `Registry-first operational scan of Africa-site malaria randomized trials: identifying lean transfer templates for faster trial delivery`, for publication in `Malaria Journal`.

This manuscript uses public ClinicalTrials.gov records to identify completed, results-posted malaria randomized trials with at least one African site and to estimate how often these studies resemble operationally lean templates for future African RCT delivery. In the benchmark set, `27` of `80` Africa-site malaria trials met predefined shortlist criteria for smaller operational footprint and prospective registration, corresponding to a proportion of `0.34` with `95% confidence interval 0.24 to 0.45`. A malaria-specific deep dive further showed that the most transferable examples were more often mixed African-site and external-sponsor studies than clearly locally sponsored studies in registry metadata alone.

We believe the manuscript is a strong fit for `Malaria Journal` because the journal explicitly covers malaria operational research, intervention studies, and health-system or planning questions. The submission also includes a public reproducibility package with the analyzed CSV outputs, code used to generate the benchmark, a concise E156 summary, and a protocol-style methods companion.
